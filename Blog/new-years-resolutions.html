New Year's Resolutions

13 FEB 2023

It was 2:00 p.m. on a Friday when I got the email. Congratulations — you've been matched with a job for the upcoming work term! I was not expecting this at all. I already mentally prepared myself for a rejection and revised my resume the day before to continue the job hunt. Instead, I found myself ranked a 1 in Cycle 1 of my 1B term for a very desirable placement (If you aren’t a Waterloo student, sorry for the incomprehensible sentence you just had to read). I’m very proud of myself, but evidently, Fortune was also on my side.

For the entirety of this term, I have just been doing things on autopilot. “I need to do my assignments so I can do my projects. I need to do my projects so I can put them on my resume and get interviews. I need to get interviews so I can land a co-op placement.” And that honestly worked quite well. For the better part of five weeks, I stayed ahead of school work, worked on side projects, practiced competitive programming, and hung out with friends often enough that I never felt close to burning out.

It is now 11:00 p.m. the next Monday. Most nights, I would spend an hour doing non-academic coding, and another hour reading, before sleeping at 12:00 a.m. However, since receiving that job offer, I have been neglecting this bedtime routine. Tonight, I was again unable to bring myself to do either of these things, even though I used to genuinely look forward to these final two hours of my day.

I would consider myself to be a pretty disciplined person. The philosophy I always had when working towards a goal is just to try as hard as I can and see how far I can go. And this worked because the primal part of me was genuinely curious to see how far I could go. Not anymore. Of course, my rational self still believes we have better things to do than watch YouTube all day, but upon hearing of my job offer the procrastination monster has come out of its grave and decided that watching stupid videos is exactly how I want to spend my day.

After some contemplation, I have decided to try something new—to quantify my goals in what I believe are commonly referred to as New Year’s Resolutions. It’s a well known saying that people overestimate what they can accomplish in a year and underestimate what they can accomplish in 10 years. But since I have no idea what I want to be doing in 10 years, I will now proceed to overestimate what I hope to accomplish in the rest of this calendar year. Worst case scenario, I fall short but still make some progress. Best case scenario, I surprise myself. Without further ado, here are my “New Year’s” Resolutions:

  1. Read 20 books. I’m on my 4th right now.
  2. Build a project with the MERN stack. While I don’t see web development as an end goal, I have some cool ideas in mind also just think it’s a really neat skill to have.
  3. Develop and implement a machine learning trading algorithm. This one is definitely on the ambitious side, and I’m not even sure how I will even begin to approach this.
  4. Learn 10 pieces of repertoire on the piano. I’ve neglected many amazing Chopin pieces over the past few years, so I’m hoping to learn some of his Etudes and whatever else sounds cool.
“It is good to have an end to journey toward; but it is the journey that matters, in the end.” — Ernest Hemingway
